# The Protectors

Semi-divine beings appointed to maintain the balance between Brighthaven, Shadewoven, and the Living World.

---

## Nature

The Protectors are not gods in the traditional sense — they are beings elevated from the cosmos itself to serve a specific function: the maintenance of the Veil and the balance between the planes. They are ancient, powerful, and partially bound by the covenant that created them.

Each Protector has a primary alignment (light or shadow) but all are expected to serve the whole system, not their aligned plane alone.

---

## The Covenant

The Protectors operate under a covenant — a set of metaphysical obligations that govern their behavior and limits. The covenant includes:

- Non-interference in mortal politics except when the Veil is threatened
- Obligation to maintain the Veil collaboratively — no Protector acts alone on major changes
- Prohibition against permanently claiming mortal souls for their aligned plane
- Accountability to the collective — decisions affecting the balance require consensus

The Betrayer's actions were a violation of this covenant, and the consequences of that violation — the Inversion — are ongoing.

## The Betrayer's Impact

The Betrayer did not simply break the covenant — they took actions that actively destabilized the Veil. The nature of those actions is a major plot mystery. What is known:

- The Inversion began at the moment of the Betrayal
- Other Protectors were unable or unwilling to immediately reverse the damage
- The Betrayer still exists and acts in the world, though their goals are not straightforwardly destructive

---

## Protectors and Mortals

Protectors rarely interact directly with mortals. When they do, it is significant. Solas Aghrian's spiritlock appears to connect them to Protector-level events in a way that is unusual and unexplained in current canon.
