# Story Arc: Inversion of Light and Shadow

The central story arc of the tSAC campaign.

---

## Arc Overview

The Inversion of Light and Shadow is the overarching conflict of the tSAC story. It encompasses the entire campaign and provides the stakes against which all other events are measured.

**The core question:** Can the Inversion be stopped — and if so, at what cost, and by whom?

---

## Setup

The Inversion began at the moment of the Betrayal, when a shadow-aligned Protector violated the covenant and took unilateral action that destabilized the Veil. The long-term consequence is a slow collapse of the light/shadow boundary that, if completed, would dissolve the structure of the three-realm cosmos.

At the story's opening, the Inversion is visibly accelerating. Institutions are strained. Lost souls are multiplying. Shadebound individuals are appearing at higher rates. The factions that should be responding are fractured.

Into this situation: Solas Aghrian, a mortal with an unusual spiritlock who is drawn into the conflict at a level no ordinary mortal should reach.

---

## Arc Structure

### Act One: Emergence

**Theme:** The mortal discovers the shape of a cosmic problem.

Key beats:
- Solas becomes aware that their spiritlock is drawing unusual attention
- First contact with the Veil Guard — instrumentalizing approach
- Introduction to Loughan Begarrow and the spy network
- First encounter with a lost soul or Corrupted Guard element that reveals the Inversion's local effects
- A significant Veil anomaly event that forces Solas into a decision

**Act One Question:** *What am I involved in, and can I walk away?*

---

### Act Two: Entanglement

**Theme:** The mortal is fully drawn in; no clean exit; choices start to define them.

Key beats:
- First contact with the Betrayer — nature of contact TBD
- Revelation that the spiritlock connects to the Betrayer's actions in some way
- Veil Guard relationship deteriorates or shifts — they want more than Solas will give
- Loughan Begarrow faces a choice that defines their arc
- The Corrupted Guard becomes a direct threat

**Act Two Question:** *What am I willing to do, and for whom?*

---

### Act Three: Inversion

**Theme:** The climax — choices made in act two have consequences; the Inversion reaches a critical point.

Key beats:
- The Betrayer's true motivation is revealed
- Solas must use the spiritlock in a way that has permanent consequences
- The Inversion either escalates to crisis or is arrested — the shape of either outcome is defined by choices made in Act Two
- The Betrayer's fate is not predetermined — it depends on player choices and Solas's relationship with them

**Act Three Question:** *What does it cost to hold the world together — and who gets to decide?*

---

## Thematic Core

- **Individual vs. cosmic system** — What does one mortal owe to a system they didn't choose and didn't build?
- **Betrayal and justification** — When is violation of a broken covenant itself a form of integrity?
- **The cost of consequence** — Real consequences are heavy; bearing them is what makes choices meaningful
