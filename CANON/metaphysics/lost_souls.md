# Lost Souls

Souls that have become untethered from both planes — adrift between Brighthaven and Shadewoven.

---

## Definition

A lost soul is a soul that has failed to complete its natural migration after death — or, in rarer cases, has been severed from its plane-anchor while still nominally alive. Lost souls are neither integrated into Brighthaven as soul-echoes nor dissolved into Shadewoven as potential. They persist in a state of incomplete transition.

---

## How Souls Become Lost

The most common causes:

- **Spiritlock failure** — A spiritlock that cannot be resolved prevents migration; if the soul deteriorates, it becomes lost rather than waiting
- **Veil disruption** — A destabilized Veil can interrupt migration mid-transit
- **Violent or traumatic death near a spirit-thin place** — Extreme energetic disruption at the moment of death
- **Inversion effects** — The ongoing Inversion is producing lost souls at increasing frequency as plane-alignment becomes unstable

---

## Nature of Lost Souls

Lost souls are not fully coherent entities. Their defining characteristics:

- **Fragmented memory** — They retain pieces of their life but not the whole
- **Incomplete form** — Their soul-structure has begun to degrade without plane-anchor support
- **Resonance** — They are drawn to places or people connected to their original life or to their incomplete state
- **Instability** — The longer a soul remains lost, the less coherent it becomes

Lost souls are not malevolent by nature, but their instability makes them unpredictable and potentially dangerous — particularly to shadebound individuals or those carrying spiritlocks, with whom they may attempt to anchor.

---

## Resolution of Lost Souls

A lost soul can be resolved through:

- **Spiritlock completion** — If the soul was lost due to a spiritlock, resolving the lock allows migration to resume
- **Plane-anchor ritual** — Aligning the soul's remaining form with its dominant plane, allowing it to complete the migration
- **Dissolution** — If the soul has degraded too far, it may dissolve into unstructured energy rather than integrating cleanly — a form of true death with no continuity

The Veil Guard has protocols for lost soul resolution, but the Inversion is overwhelming their capacity.

---

## The Inversion and Lost Souls

The rising number of lost souls is one of the most visible signs of the Inversion's progress in the Living World. It functions as both a practical problem (unstable entities accumulating) and a symbolic indicator: the system that gives death meaning is failing.
