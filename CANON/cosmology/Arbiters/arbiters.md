# Arbiters

Arbiters are individuals infused with both **[Brighthaven](../energies/brighthaven.md)** and **[Shadewoven](../energies/shadewoven.md)** energy.

They act as agents of the **[Concordance](../concordance.md)**, preserving equilibrium between the two forces that govern reality.

Unlike ordinary mortals, Arbiters exist partially within both the physical world and the deeper **[ley-line lattice](../ley_lines.md)** that connects reality to the Concordance.

Their purpose is not to enforce morality or political order.

Their sole responsibility is **balance**.

---

# Creation

Arbiters are almost always former mortals.

When an individual’s life intersects strongly with the forces of equilibrium — through sacrifice, discovery, catastrophe, or profound influence on history — the **[Concordance](../concordance.md)** may elevate that individual.

During this transformation, both **[Brighthaven](../energies/brighthaven.md)** and **[Shadewoven](../energies/shadewoven.md)** energies bind to the individual.

This grants the ability to perceive and interact with the **[ley-line lattice](../ley_lines.md)**.

The exact mechanism by which individuals are chosen remains poorly understood even by Arbiters themselves.

---

# Nature of an Arbiter

Arbiters exist in a state between mortal and cosmic being.

They retain their identity and personality while gaining awareness of the deeper structure of reality.

### Core Traits

Arbiters:

- do not age
- retain mortal memories
- can still be destroyed
- perceive disturbances within the **[ley-line lattice](../ley_lines.md)**
- can shift perception toward the **[Concordance](../concordance.md)**

---

# Perception

Arbiters perceive the world differently from mortals.

Where mortals see only geography and events, Arbiters perceive:

- flows of **[Brighthaven](../energies/brighthaven.md)** energy  
- flows of **[Shadewoven](../energies/shadewoven.md)** energy  
- disturbances within the **[ley-line lattice](../ley_lines.md)**  
- scars created by past equilibrium disruptions

---

# Arbiter Roles

Arbiters naturally specialize in particular types of equilibrium work.

These roles are not rigid ranks but functional divisions.

---

## Examiners

Observers who monitor long-term patterns across civilizations and the **[ley-line lattice](../ley_lines.md)**.

They identify emerging disturbances before they manifest physically.

---

## Inquisitors

Investigators who analyze disturbances already affecting the mortal world.

They determine whether intervention is necessary.

---

## Reclaimers

Retrieval specialists.

They recover artifacts, entities, or consciousness fragments that threaten equilibrium.

Many Reclaimers operate quietly within mortal societies.

---

## Justicars

Combat specialists deployed when equilibrium collapses into open conflict.

In mortal history they are often remembered as legendary heroes.

---

## Wardens

Guardians of Concordance vaults.

These vaults store dangerous artifacts, imprisoned entities, and historical records tied to disturbances in the **[Concordance](../concordance.md)**.

Wardens rarely leave their domains.

---

# Coordination

Although Arbiters operate independently, their efforts are coordinated by the **[Planewright](planewright.md)**.

The Planewright interprets large-scale disturbances within the **[Concordance](../concordance.md)** and guides Arbiter response when necessary.

---

# The Null

When equilibrium collapses beyond Arbiter control, the **[Concordance](../concordance.md)** may deploy entities known as the **[Null](null.md)**.

These beings represent catastrophic system correction mechanisms.

The appearance of a Null indicates that normal Arbiter intervention has failed.

---

# Rarity

Arbiters are extremely rare.

Too many agents with such power would destabilize the very equilibrium they are meant to preserve.

For this reason the **[Concordance](../concordance.md)** acts with extreme restraint when creating new Arbiters.
