# The Veil Guard

The faction charged with maintaining the Veil and regulating the boundary between the Living World and the cosmic planes.

---

## Overview

The Veil Guard is the primary institutional body responsible for monitoring and maintaining the integrity of the Veil — the metaphysical boundary between the Living World and the planes of Brighthaven and Shadewoven. They operate with semi-official authority in the Living World, recognized by most mortal institutions without being formally part of any one of them.

---

## Structure and Organization

The Veil Guard is organized into:

- **The Council** — Senior members who set policy and adjudicate major decisions
- **Field Wardens** — Operational agents who monitor spirit-thin places and respond to Veil anomalies
- **Analysts** — Those who study patterns, track soul anomalies, and model the Inversion's progress
- **Ritual Specialists** — Practitioners who perform lost soul resolutions, spiritlock assessments, and Veil reinforcement work

The Veil Guard answers, formally, to the Protectors' covenant — but with the Betrayer's fall and the covenant in question, the Council has been operating with increasing autonomy.

---

## Mission and Methods

Core mission: **Maintain the Veil. Prevent plane-bleed. Resolve soul anomalies.**

Methods include:
- Monitoring spirit-thin places
- Tracking shadebound individuals (often without those individuals' knowledge)
- Performing lost soul resolution rituals
- Coordinating with the spy network on intelligence about Veil threats
- Suppressing information about the Inversion's severity to prevent panic

---

## Relationship to Other Factions

| Faction | Relationship |
|---------|-------------|
| Spy Network | Uneasy alliance — they share intelligence but have different priorities |
| Corrupted Guard | Former colleagues; the Veil Guard considers them a primary threat |
| Protectors | Nominally subordinate; in practice increasingly independent |

---

## Veil Guard and Solas Aghrian

The Veil Guard is aware of Solas and their spiritlock. They view Solas as a significant asset and a significant risk — an individual with Protector-level soul connectivity is both a tool and a potential Veil disruption waiting to happen. Their approach to Solas is therefore instrumentalizing: they want to use Solas without necessarily protecting Solas's interests.
