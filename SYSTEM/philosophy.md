# System Philosophy

The core design philosophy of the tSAC game system.

This document describes the principles that guide how mechanics should be designed, interpreted, and expanded. Any future rules, systems, or subsystems should align with the philosophy described here.

The purpose of this philosophy is to ensure that the mechanics reinforce the themes and metaphysics of the tSAC universe rather than competing with them.

---

# Design Intent

The tSAC game system is built to **serve the story and the setting**, not the other way around.

Mechanics exist to support narrative and cosmology rather than replacing them with abstract rule structures.

The system aims to accomplish several goals:

1. **Make consequences feel real and durable**  
   Violence, mistakes, and difficult choices should leave lasting effects.

2. **Create meaningful decisions under pressure**  
   Players should face situations where every choice has a cost.

3. **Reflect the metaphysical structure of the world**  
   The mechanics should mirror the cosmological rules that govern the setting.

4. **Reward engagement with the lore**  
   Players who understand the world should be able to interact with it more effectively.

In other words, the system is designed to act as a **framework for dramatic storytelling grounded in a consistent cosmology**.

---

# Core Principles

## Consequences Are Durable

Actions should have lasting effects.

In many systems, characters can recover quickly between encounters, effectively resetting the game state. tSAC intentionally avoids this pattern.

In this system:

- Injuries persist until properly treated
- Exhaustion accumulates through Stamina loss
- Psychological strain affects Willpower
- Resources must be managed carefully

When characters suffer harm or loss, it should remain meaningful within the narrative.

This principle ensures that danger feels real and that success carries weight.

---

## Resource Tension Is the Primary Driver

The system is structured around two core resources:

- **Stamina** — physical capacity to continue acting
- **Willpower** — mental and spiritual resilience

Most encounters revolve around managing these resources effectively.

Characters are rarely attempting to find the mathematically optimal action. Instead, they are making decisions under pressure about how to spend their limited capacity to endure.

This resource tension creates the dramatic engine of the system.

## Narrative and Mechanic Are Integrated

Mechanical states should correspond closely with narrative reality.

If a character is exhausted, injured, terrified, or spiritually compromised, the mechanics should reflect that state.

Likewise, narrative conditions should influence mechanical outcomes.

Examples include:

- A wounded character fighting less effectively
- A shadebound character experiencing altered metaphysical interactions
- A character suffering trauma reacting differently to stress

The system avoids allowing mechanical abstractions to drift away from the story.

Mechanics exist to **express the narrative state of the world**, not replace it.

---

## The Supernatural Is Not Routine

Supernatural phenomena should feel fundamentally different from mundane challenges.

Encounters involving:

- Brighthaven
- Shadewoven
- Protectors
- lost souls
- Veil distortions
- spiritlock events

should not simply function as harder versions of normal encounters.

They should introduce **qualitatively different experiences**, often involving Willpower, Attunement, or metaphysical consequences.

This reinforces the idea that the cosmological forces shaping the universe are **significant and rare**, not everyday obstacles.

---

# Anti-Patterns to Avoid

Certain design patterns undermine the intended feel of the system.

| Anti-Pattern | Why It Undermines the Design |
|---|---|
| Reliable healing between every encounter | Removes the durability of consequences and reduces tension |
| Binary success/failure with no cost on success | Eliminates meaningful tradeoffs and resource management |
| Mechanics that ignore narrative state | Creates a disconnect between the story and the rules |
| Supernatural encounters treated like mundane ones | Diminishes the cosmological importance of these events |

Avoiding these patterns helps maintain the tone and stakes of the setting.

---

# Relationship to Canon

The game system exists within the metaphysical framework of the tSAC universe.

When mechanical systems interact with concepts such as:

- souls
- spiritlock
- shadebound conditions
- Veil distortions
- Protector-level entities

they must remain consistent with the definitions established in the canonical lore.

See:

`CANON/metaphysics/`

If a mechanical system conflicts with established canon, one of two things must happen:

1. The mechanic must be revised.
2. The conflict must be flagged for discussion regarding canon.

This rule ensures that the **mechanical layer of the system remains aligned with the narrative and cosmological layer**.
