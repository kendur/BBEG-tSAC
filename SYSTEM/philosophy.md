# System Philosophy

The core design philosophy of the tSAC game system.

---

## Design Intent

The tSAC game system is built to serve the story and the setting, not the other way around. Mechanics exist to:

1. Make consequences feel real and durable
2. Create meaningful decisions under pressure
3. Reflect the metaphysical structure of the tSAC world in gameplay
4. Reward player engagement with the lore

---

## Core Principles

### Consequences Are Durable

Death, injury, and loss matter. The system does not offer easy reset buttons. When a character is hurt, they stay hurt unless they have time and resources to recover. When something is lost, it is lost. This creates genuine stakes.

### Resource Tension Is the Primary Driver

The system uses two key resources — Stamina and Willpower — to create decisions. Most encounters are about managing these resources intelligently, not finding the optimal mechanical solution. See [Stamina and Willpower](stamina_willpower.md).

### Narrative and Mechanic Are Integrated

Mechanical states should reflect narrative reality and vice versa. A character who is exhausted fights like they're exhausted. A character who is shadebound has that reality reflected in their capabilities and vulnerabilities. The game should not allow mechanical states to drift from narrative ones.

### The Supernatural Is Not Routine

Contact with Brighthaven, Shadewoven, Protectors, or lost souls is significant. The system should not make these encounters feel routine or mechanically identical to mundane challenges. They should be marked as different in kind, not just difficulty.

---

## Anti-Patterns to Avoid

| Anti-Pattern | Why It Undermines the Design |
|---|---|
| Reliable healing between every encounter | Undercuts the durability of consequences |
| Binary success/failure with no cost on success | Removes resource tension |
| Mechanics that ignore narrative state | Creates disconnect between story and game |
| Supernatural treated identically to mundane | Flattens the cosmological significance |

---

## Relationship to Canon

System mechanics should be consistent with the metaphysics defined in `CANON/metaphysics/`. Where mechanics touch on soul mechanics, shadebound, spiritlock, or Veil interactions, the canonical definitions take precedence. If a mechanical need conflicts with canon, resolve it by revising the mechanic or flagging for canon discussion.
