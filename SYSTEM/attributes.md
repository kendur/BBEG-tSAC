# Attributes

Character attributes and their meaning in the tSAC system.

---

# Overview

Attributes represent a character’s **fundamental capabilities**. They define the core domains of human ability from which all other actions, mechanics, and derived values are calculated.

The tSAC system intentionally uses **a small set of attributes**. Rather than dividing abilities into dozens of narrow categories, the system focuses on deeper interaction between a few broad domains of capability.

This approach keeps characters easy to understand while still allowing for meaningful differentiation between individuals.

Attributes represent **innate capability and trained potential**, not specific learned skills.

---

# Design Philosophy

The attribute system is built on several principles:

• **Clarity over granularity** – A small number of meaningful attributes is easier to reason about than a large number of narrowly defined ones.

• **Interaction over isolation** – Many mechanical outcomes are derived from combinations of attributes rather than a single stat acting alone.

• **Narrative grounding** – Attributes represent understandable aspects of a character rather than abstract game mechanics.

Because of this design philosophy, attributes form the **foundation layer** of the system. Most other mechanics derive from them rather than existing independently.

# The Core Attributes

| Attribute | Description | Associated With |
|-----------|-------------|-----------------|
| **Strength** | Physical power, endurance, and the ability to apply force to the world. | Stamina pools, lifting, grappling, sustained physical effort |
| **Agility** | Speed, coordination, reflexes, and bodily precision. | Defense, ranged actions, evasion, rapid movement |
| **Wit** | Intelligence, reasoning ability, memory, and pattern recognition. | Knowledge checks, investigation, tactical planning |
| **Resolve** | Mental endurance, determination, and emotional stability under pressure. | Willpower pools, resisting fear, resisting coercion or supernatural influence |
| **Presence** | Social force and personal gravitas. Presence represents how strongly a character affects the perceptions and emotions of others. | Persuasion, intimidation, leadership |
| **Attunement** | Sensitivity to metaphysical forces and spiritual phenomena. | Interacting with Veil phenomena, detecting supernatural disturbances, spirit interactions |

---

# Attribute Ratings

Attributes are rated on a scale from **1 to 5**.

| Rating | Description |
|------|-------------|
| 1 | Below average capability for the general population |
| 2 | Average adult capability |
| 3 | Clearly capable and reliable in this domain |
| 4 | Exceptional ability beyond normal expectations |
| 5 | Among the most capable mortals possible |

Most starting characters possess a mixture of **2s and 3s**, with one or two attributes reaching **4** to represent areas of specialization.

A rating of **5** represents the upper bounds of mortal capability.

---

# Attunement as a Special Case

Attunement is unique among the attributes because it governs interaction with **metaphysical systems** rather than purely physical or psychological domains.

Attunement represents how strongly an individual resonates with the deeper forces that shape reality.

This includes sensitivity to:

- Veil distortions
- Spirit activity
- Shadebound entities
- Spiritlock conditions
- Ley-line disturbances

High Attunement can be both beneficial and dangerous.

Characters with strong Attunement are more capable of detecting supernatural forces, but they are also **more exposed to them**. Phenomena that remain unnoticed by others may affect them directly.

This makes Attunement a powerful but potentially hazardous trait.

---

# Derived Values

Several important gameplay values are derived from combinations of attributes.

| Derived Value | Derivation |
|--------------|------------|
| **Stamina (max)** | Based on Strength + Resolve |
| **Willpower (max)** | Based on Resolve + Attunement |
| **Defense Rating** | Based on Agility + situational modifiers |
| **Initiative** | Based on Agility + Wit |

These derived values represent **practical outcomes of capability** rather than separate independent attributes.

For example:

- Stamina reflects the interaction of **physical strength and mental endurance**.
- Willpower reflects the interaction of **mental resilience and metaphysical sensitivity**.

This layered design keeps the attribute system compact while still allowing for meaningful mechanical depth.
