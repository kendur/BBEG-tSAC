# Stamina and Willpower

The two primary resource mechanics of the tSAC system.

---

## Overview

Stamina and Willpower are the central resource pools in tSAC. Most meaningful actions and encounters are framed in terms of these two resources. Managing them is the primary skill expression of the system.

They are not simply "hit points." They track different dimensions of a character's capacity and interact differently with different threats.

---

## Stamina

**Stamina represents physical and vitality capacity** — the ability to keep acting, absorb punishment, and sustain effort.

### Stamina Pool

- Maximum Stamina = (Strength + Resolve) × 3
- Stamina refreshes through rest (see [Injury Model](injury_model.md) for full recovery mechanics)
- Stamina at 0 = the character is incapacitated (unable to take physical actions)

### Stamina Loss Sources

| Source | Stamina Effect |
|--------|---------------|
| Physical attacks | Direct Stamina damage (modified by armor/Defense) |
| Sustained exertion | Gradual Stamina drain over time |
| Severe environmental hazards | Variable Stamina damage |
| Certain supernatural effects | May affect Stamina as well as Willpower |

### Stamina and Injuries

Stamina tracks current capacity, but injuries track lasting damage. A character can recover Stamina to maximum and still carry injuries that affect their capability. See [Injury Model](injury_model.md).

---

## Willpower

**Willpower represents mental, emotional, and spiritual capacity** — the ability to act under pressure, resist supernatural influence, and maintain self against cosmic forces.

### Willpower Pool

- Maximum Willpower = (Resolve + Attunement) × 3
- Willpower refreshes through meaningful rest and recovery of purpose (not just sleep)
- Willpower at 0 = the character is unable to act voluntarily; they are overwhelmed, frozen, or dissociated

### Willpower Loss Sources

| Source | Willpower Effect |
|--------|-----------------|
| Supernatural encounters | Variable Willpower drain depending on intensity |
| Moral/psychological shock | Willpower loss on significant moral violations or witnessing horror |
| Shadebound instability | Willpower drain during episodes of shadow-fragment disruption |
| Spiritlock resonance events | Variable; can be positive or negative depending on circumstances |
| Extended fear or pressure | Gradual Willpower drain under sustained stress |

### Willpower and the Supernatural

Because Willpower is tied to Attunement, characters with high Attunement have larger Willpower pools — but also encounter more Willpower-threatening situations. The supernatural costs more for those sensitive to it.

---

## Resource Interaction

Stamina and Willpower are related but separate:

- A character with 0 Stamina but high Willpower may be unable to act physically but can still think, decide, and resist
- A character with 0 Willpower but high Stamina may be physically functional but paralyzed or compelled
- Both at 0 is a total incapacitation scenario — a character in this state requires significant external intervention
