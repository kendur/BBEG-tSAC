# Stamina and Willpower

The two primary resource mechanics of the tSAC system.

These two pools form the **central tension engine** of gameplay. Most encounters and decisions revolve around how characters manage their remaining capacity to continue acting.

Unlike traditional hit-point systems, Stamina and Willpower represent **different dimensions of endurance**, each responding to different kinds of threats.

---

# Overview

Stamina and Willpower track two fundamental aspects of a character's ability to remain active in difficult situations.

- **Stamina** represents physical vitality and the ability to continue exerting the body.
- **Willpower** represents mental, emotional, and spiritual resilience.

Together they define the limits of what a character can endure.

Managing these resources effectively is the primary skill expression of the system.

Characters rarely fail simply because they lack ability. Instead, they fail when they **run out of capacity to continue**.

---

# Stamina

**Stamina represents physical endurance and vitality.**

It measures a character's ability to:

- absorb physical punishment
- maintain physical exertion
- continue acting while injured or exhausted
- recover breath and composure under strain

Stamina does **not** represent permanent injury. It represents the temporary capacity to keep functioning.

---

## Stamina Pool

Maximum Stamina is determined by a character's physical and mental endurance.

**Maximum Stamina = (Strength + Resolve) × 3**

Strength contributes raw physical durability, while Resolve reflects the determination to continue despite fatigue or pain.

---

## Stamina Recovery

Stamina is primarily restored through **rest and reduced exertion**.

Recovery may occur through:

- short rest periods after exertion
- extended rest and sleep
- medical treatment that stabilizes physical strain

Stamina recovery does **not remove injuries**. A character may have full Stamina while still suffering significant injury penalties.

For lasting damage, see:  
[Injury Model](injury_model.md)

---

## Stamina Loss Sources

| Source | Stamina Effect |
|------|----------------|
| Physical attacks | Direct Stamina damage modified by armor and Defense |
| Sustained exertion | Gradual Stamina drain over time |
| Severe environmental hazards | Variable Stamina damage |
| Certain supernatural effects | May affect both Stamina and Willpower |

Stamina loss represents exhaustion, shock, breath loss, and the body's struggle to continue functioning.

---

## Stamina and Injuries

Stamina tracks **current operating capacity**.

Injuries represent **lasting physical damage**.

A character may restore Stamina fully and still carry injuries that impair their ability to fight or perform physical actions.

This separation prevents the unrealistic cycle where characters fully recover between encounters.

See:  
[Injury Model](injury_model.md)

---

# Willpower

**Willpower represents mental, emotional, and spiritual resilience.**

It measures a character's ability to:

- remain composed under extreme stress
- resist supernatural influence
- maintain identity and self-control
- confront fear, horror, or metaphysical pressure

Where Stamina represents the body's limits, Willpower represents the **mind and spirit's limits**.

---

## Willpower Pool

Maximum Willpower is determined by a character's resilience and metaphysical sensitivity.

**Maximum Willpower = (Resolve + Attunement) × 3**

Resolve provides emotional endurance, while Attunement reflects the character's connection to metaphysical forces.

---

## Willpower Recovery

Willpower does **not recover as easily as Stamina**.

While physical exhaustion may fade with sleep, psychological and spiritual strain often requires deeper recovery.

Willpower recovery may require:

- meaningful rest away from stress
- emotional support or companionship
- reaffirmation of personal purpose
- spiritual or psychological healing

This design prevents Willpower from becoming a disposable resource.

---

## Willpower Loss Sources

| Source | Willpower Effect |
|------|-----------------|
| Supernatural encounters | Variable Willpower drain depending on intensity |
| Moral or psychological shock | Loss triggered by witnessing or committing horrific acts |
| Shadebound instability | Willpower drain during shadow-fragment disruptions |
| Spiritlock resonance events | Variable effects depending on the interaction |
| Sustained fear or pressure | Gradual Willpower drain under prolonged stress |

These sources represent situations where a character's mental stability or sense of identity is threatened.

---

## Willpower and the Supernatural

Because Willpower interacts strongly with **Attunement**, characters who are highly attuned to metaphysical forces often experience both advantages and risks.

High Attunement may grant:

- greater perception of supernatural phenomena
- stronger interaction with metaphysical systems

However, it may also expose the character to:

- stronger Willpower pressure
- more intense supernatural encounters
- deeper spiritual consequences

The supernatural therefore becomes **more significant**, not merely easier.

---

# Resource Interaction

Stamina and Willpower interact but remain distinct.

Examples:

- A character with **0 Stamina but high Willpower** may remain mentally clear but physically incapable of acting.
- A character with **0 Willpower but high Stamina** may still be physically capable but mentally unable to act freely.
- A character with **both resources depleted** is fully incapacitated.

These edge cases allow the system to represent situations such as:

- physical collapse despite strong determination
- psychological breakdown despite physical strength
- supernatural domination of otherwise capable individuals

---

# Relationship to System Philosophy

The Stamina and Willpower model embodies the central principles of the tSAC system:

- consequences accumulate
- endurance is limited
- decisions under pressure matter

Encounters are therefore not simply about winning or losing a fight, but about **how much of a character's remaining capacity must be spent to survive the situation**.
