# Combat Resolution

How combat is structured and resolved in the tSAC system.

---

## Design Intent

Combat in tSAC is designed to:

- Be dangerous and consequential (aligned with the [System Philosophy](philosophy.md))
- Create meaningful tactical decisions without requiring complex bookkeeping
- Reflect the physical and psychological toll of violence on both sides
- End in ways that are narratively satisfying rather than just numerically determined

---

## Combat Structure

### Initiative

At the start of combat, each participant rolls initiative: **Agility + Wit** (2d6 + modifier). High initiative acts first within a round.

### The Action Economy

Each round, a character may take:

- **One primary action** — attack, complex maneuver, use a skill, interact with an environmental element
- **One secondary action** — move, draw a weapon, communicate a brief command, simple reactive maneuver
- **One reaction** — used in response to another character's action (defense, dodge, opportunistic attack)

Reactions are spent; if a character has used their reaction, they cannot make another until their next turn.

---

## Attacks and Defense

### Making an Attack

Roll: **Relevant Attribute** (typically Strength for melee, Agility for ranged) + **2d6**

Compare to the target's **Defense Rating** (Agility + armor + situational modifiers).

| Result | Outcome |
|--------|---------|
| Below Defense | Miss — no effect |
| Equal to Defense | Glancing hit — reduced effect |
| Above Defense | Hit — full effect |
| 5+ above Defense | Clean hit — enhanced effect |

### Damage

A hit deals Stamina damage based on:

- Weapon base damage
- The degree of success (glancing/full/clean)
- Any relevant attribute bonuses

---

## Stamina and Combat

When Stamina falls:

| Stamina Level | Effect |
|---------------|--------|
| 75–100% | No penalty |
| 50–74% | Minor penalty to complex actions |
| 25–49% | Moderate penalty; reactions cost effort |
| 1–24% | Significant penalty; acting takes Willpower to maintain |
| 0 | Incapacitated; cannot act without external intervention |

---

## Willpower in Combat

Willpower does not typically change in mundane combat, but:

- **Horrific violence** — witnessing or committing atrocity may require a Willpower check
- **Supernatural combat** — fighting shadebound, lost souls, or entities with metaphysical dimension uses Willpower as well as Stamina
- **Protector-adjacent events** — any direct encounter with a Protector or Protector-level energy in a combat context is a Willpower event

---

## Combat End

Combat ends when:

- One side is incapacitated, surrenders, or flees
- The tactical situation is resolved (objective achieved or lost)
- An external event changes the context

**Note:** Incapacitation is not death. The system treats death as a specific outcome that requires specific circumstances — deliberate killing blow, catastrophic injury without treatment, or specific narrative conditions. See [Injury Model](injury_model.md).
